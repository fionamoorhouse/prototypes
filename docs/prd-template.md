## Example: HelloFresh Cookbook

Here's what a filled-in version looks like from a real project:

**The Problem:**
HelloFresh users discover recipes on Instagram, TikTok, and food blogs but have no way to save them into HelloFresh for later. Recipes get lost in bookmarks and screenshots. Users want a personal collection they can browse when deciding what to cook.

**Who Experiences This?**
HelloFresh subscribers who are active on social media and food content. They find recipes outside the app throughout the week, then forget about them when it's time to plan meals.

**What Happens Today?**
Users screenshot recipes or use their phone's bookmark feature. These are scattered across apps with no organization. When it's time to cook, they can't find what they saved or remember why they saved it.

**Reference & Inspiration:**
NYT Cooking app — the way it lets you save recipes into custom collections (cookbooks) with a clean grid view. Pinterest boards for the folder/collection model.

**Constraints or Non-goals:**
No actual recipe import from URLs (that's a backend feature). Focus on the save, organize, and browse experience. Don't need real data — placeholders are fine.

**Initial Ideas:**
Some kind of "Cookbook" section in the app where users can create folders/collections and save recipes into them. Maybe a quick-save action right from a recipe card.
